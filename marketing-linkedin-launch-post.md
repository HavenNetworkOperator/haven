# LinkedIn Launch Post — Founder Story

**Goal:** Reflective, professional register. Builds founder credibility with the parent/tech/policy audience LinkedIn rewards. Single long-form post — no carousel, no document attached.
**Length:** ~1,420 characters (well under LinkedIn's 3,000-char limit; long enough to land, short enough to read in one breath).
**Best time (UK):** Tuesday or Thursday, 7:30am or 12:30pm.
**Image:** Founder headshot if available. If not, the Haven hero image (paper background, terracotta logo mark) works.

---

## Post body

> Every UK mobile network treats a 14-year-old the same as a 40-year-old.
>
> That's not a feature. That's a design failure — and it's the one I've spent the last several months trying to fix.
>
> Today I'm sharing what I'm building. It's called Haven, and it's Britain's first mobile network designed around families.
>
> The Smartphone Free Childhood movement has done something extraordinary. Hundreds of thousands of UK parents who've delayed giving their child a phone, and a national conversation about what social media is doing to a generation. But "delay forever" isn't a real answer. Eventually our children get a phone. The harder, more honest question is: *what does responsible look like then?*
>
> Haven is my attempt at that answer.
>
> Safety lives at the SIM layer — not in an app a child can uninstall in thirty seconds. Adult content, VPNs and proxies are blocked at the network. AI safety runs on-device, watching for grooming patterns and age-inappropriate content in real time, while messages themselves stay private. Parents get a weekly summary, not a surveillance feed.
>
> I'm not a telco lifer. I'm a parent — building this with other parents from the SFC community because nobody designing mobile networks today treats children as the primary user.
>
> No VC. No growth-at-all-costs. Funding it through Kickstarter and equity crowdfunding so the people building it are the people using it.
>
> Targeted launch: September 2026. The first 1,000 founding members get pricing locked for life and a vote on which safety features ship first.
>
> If this is the network you've been waiting for, you'll find us at gethavenmobile.com.
>
> I'd genuinely love your honest read on this — particularly if you work in child safety, telecoms, or family policy. Reply, DM, share, push back. All of it useful.

## Comment-thread starter (drop this 30 min after the post)

> One question I keep getting: *isn't this what parental control apps already do?*
>
> Short answer — no. Apps live on the device. Anything that lives on the device can be uninstalled, sideloaded around, or simply turned off. Haven's safety lives one layer down, on the SIM and at the network. That's the whole point.
