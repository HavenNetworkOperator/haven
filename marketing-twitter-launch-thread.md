## Tweet 1 — Hook

> Every UK mobile network treats a 14-year-old the same as a 40-year-old.
>
> That's not a feature. That's a design failure.
>
> So I'm building the one that doesn't. 🧵

## Tweet 2 — The problem, named

> Teenagers are dealing with the consequences of the smartphone era every day.
>
> Grooming. Sextortion. Algorithmic addiction. Sleep destroyed by 2am scrolling.
>
> Parental control apps don't fix it — kids uninstall them in 30 seconds.
>
> The fix has to be deeper.

*(272 chars)*

---

## Tweet 3 — The insight

> The Smartphone Free Childhood movement has done something extraordinary in the UK — hundreds of thousands of parents who've delayed giving their child a phone.
>
> But "delay forever" isn't the answer. Eventually they get one.
>
> The question is: *what does responsible look like then?*

*(279 chars)*

---

## Tweet 4 — The answer

> That's Haven.
>
> Britain's first mobile network designed around families.
>
> Safety built into the SIM, not bolted on as an app. Can't be deleted. Can't be bypassed. Works on any device.

*(196 chars)*

---

## Tweet 5 — How it actually works (1)

> The safety lives at the network layer.
>
> Adult content, VPNs, and proxies are blocked by default — at the SIM, not the device. Government-grade age verification before any social media app can be installed.
>
> No app to uninstall. No setting a kid can flip.

*(266 chars)*

---

## Tweet 6 — How it actually works (2)

> The AI runs on-device. It looks for grooming patterns and age-inappropriate content in real time.
>
> Messages stay private. Parents don't get a surveillance dashboard — they get a weekly summary, and a notification only when it actually matters.

*(254 chars)*
