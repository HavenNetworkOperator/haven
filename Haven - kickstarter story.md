## Risks and challenges

Building a mobile network (even a virtual one) is genuinely hard.  
We want to be upfront about what that means.

We're a new entrant in a regulated industry.  
As an MVNO, Haven operates on top of an existing UK network infrastructure under a host network agreement.  
That agreement is being negotiated, and like any commercial relationship, it carries dependency risk. We've built contingency into our timeline and have identified alternative partners if needed.

The AI safety layer is ambitious.  
On-device detection of grooming patterns and age-inappropriate content is technically novel. We're not promising it will be perfect on day one.  
We're promising we'll ship it responsibly, test it rigorously with real families in our close network, and iterate openly.

Founding members (you\!) will get a chance to vote on which features ship first, so you're part of the prioritisation, not just the recipient of it.

Children's data regulation is strict — and it should be. The ICO's Children's Code and UK GDPR compliance requirements for under-18s are demanding. We've designed privacy-first from the start (no surveillance dashboards, weekly summaries only), but navigating regulatory approval takes time. We are moving fast, but we'd rather get this right out of the gate, than move fast and break things.

We're a small team launching something large. We're honest about what that means for timelines. If we're delayed, you'll hear from us first, with a clear reason, a revised plan, and no spin.

That's a commitment, not a platitude.

What we have that most hardware or product campaigns don't: no physical manufacturing, no supply chain, no customs risk. We're software, eSIM contracts, and network agreements. The dependencies are commercial and regulatory, not logistical.

We've thought hard about what could go wrong. The answer isn't "nothing." The answer is that we've planned for it, and we will be continuously monitoring risks.

## Use of AI

This project is not raising funds for AI tools, nor are we using content that was generated by AI. However, we will be using AI as part of a security protection mechanism to identify content and patterns of behaviour on our mobile network that pose a risk to children.
