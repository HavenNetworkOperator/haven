Our Story

Built by parents,
for families.

We are a group of non-judgemental parents who are pro-tech and pro-childhood. Here's why we're building Haven, and what we believe.

Our guiding vision is to create a world where children are free from online harms, and offline harms resulting from online influence.

The problem

Smartphones are incredible devices.

But neither they, nor the mobile phone networks they run on were designed with family safety in mind.

Teenagers are dealing with the negative impacts of social media on smartphones on a daily basis. Every mobile network treats a 14-year-old the same as a 40-year-old.

We can do better. We must do better. Which is why we are launching Haven.

Haven is a safety-first mobile phone network. Not another phone. Not another app.

Safety built in at the network layer. It can't be deleted. It can't be bypassed. It works on any device.

Our AI detects harmful patterns in real time, on-device. Messages stay private. As a parent you get notified when it matters — Haven is not a surveillance feed.

We are building this for a better future for our families and the communities they are part of.

Transparency

Risks and challenges.

Building a mobile network (even a virtual one) is genuinely hard. We want to be upfront about what that means.

We're a new entrant in a regulated industry. As an MVNO, Haven operates on top of existing UK network infrastructure under a host network agreement. That agreement is being negotiated, and like any commercial relationship, it carries dependency risk. We've built contingency into our timeline and have identified alternative partners if needed.

The AI safety layer is ambitious. On-device detection of grooming patterns and age-inappropriate content is technically novel. We're not promising it will be perfect on day one. We're promising we'll ship it responsibly, test it rigorously with real families in our close network, and iterate openly. Founding members will get a chance to vote on which features ship first.

Children's data regulation is strict — and it should be. The ICO's Children's Code and UK GDPR compliance requirements for under-18s are demanding. We've designed privacy-first from the start (no surveillance dashboards, weekly summaries only), but navigating regulatory approval takes time. We'd rather get this right out of the gate, than move fast and break things.

We're a small team launching something large. We're honest about what that means for timelines. If we're delayed, you'll hear from us first, with a clear reason, a revised plan, and no spin. That's a commitment, not a platitude.

What we have that most campaigns don't: no physical manufacturing, no supply chain, no customs risk. We're software, eSIM contracts, and network agreements. The dependencies are commercial and regulatory, not logistical.

We've thought hard about what could go wrong. The answer isn't "nothing." The answer is that we've planned for it, and we will be continuously monitoring risks.
